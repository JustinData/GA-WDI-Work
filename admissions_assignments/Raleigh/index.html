The College Years

The BFA In GD

I studied graphic design in college in an effort to combine my love of computers and my artistic talents. At first I thought I might have found a good career path since I enjoyed the medium and many other aspects of design. However, I found the overall process lacking the structure that I desired. I quickly realized that graphic design would not work long term since the subject matter was not enough to keep me engaged.

Code Lovin'

During my senior year I took a couple programming courses and found that I enjoyed the material. Learning the code was difficult but I liked having a set number of elements that I could use to construct a larger application. Thus, the innovation was in how one utilized the elements, not in the creation of them.

Post Graduation

This Could Work

A year after graduation I finally started to identify what I wanted to do in terms of a career. Much of the coding process at the beginning had been "trial and error." However, the problems or challenges I encountered while learning only increased my interest in the subject.

You Can Always Go Home

After college, I returned home to help my family and plan my next career steps. The following year I homeschooled my younger sister, completed some freelance projects, and worked as the front-end developer for a non-profit, Beyond Our Borders (BOB). Working for BOB was a great experience. I was able to implement my HTML and CSS skills as well as delve into web development on a professional level. I continued to teach myself through books and online tutorials. The more I studied, the more I wanted to learn.

Where I Am Now

Hooked on Code

I realized in order to follow my passion for web development, I needed more training so I began to look for programs in the area. Fortunately, a nearby school had a Webmaster Certification Program (WCP) in Web Development. Unfortunately, that track was not in session at the time. However, the Web Design Track was available. I decided to take the Design Track, which focused on XHTML and CSS with some JavaScript. I was surprised to discover how much information I had overlooked by teaching myself. Needless to say I was officially hooked.

Ready To Work

The WCP program finishes this week, and I can say without a doubt that I would like to be a web developer. I want to learn as much as possible in the next few months so I can start my career in web development and be confident in my coding skills.
