Hi there, I'm Mihran Abrahamian.

I grew in Geneva, Switzerland, went to high school in New York City, and attended university in Edinburgh and in Brussels.
In 2012, I moved to Montreal, where I've been working freelance as a translator and sound engineer.
I've worked on everything from end-user license agreements to commencement speeches, from medical notes to film subtitles.
In addition to recording and mixing sound for music and film, I also produce reggae music and am fascinated by Jamaican recording studio practices
of the 60s and 70s.

I recently got involved in a Toronto-based startup, Open Music Networks, completing a R&D contract which resulted in a set of
wireframes for a platform that will enable musicians to collaborate remotely on recording projects in an elegant and streamlined way.
The platform is also designed to drive shares of intellectual property to session musicians, who have tended historically to be compensated
only in flat fees.

I've spent most of my free time in the past year learning everything I could about different pieces of audio software, and studying
the workflows of audio professionals in order to help develop this product. I am now using courses online to learn the fundamentals
of coding. I'm curious, I love to tinker, and I get the hang of things very quickly.
